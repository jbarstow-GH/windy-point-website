Our Story

In 1974, Bob Barstow looked out across Lake Travis and saw possibility. He saw a place where Austin families could breathe — where weekends could taste like something worth remembering. Fifty years later, that vision never left.

Bob Barstow & The Barstow Family

Bob didn't build a business. He built a backyard — one big enough for an entire city to share. For five decades the Barstow family showed up every morning before the first visitor arrived and every evening after the last car left. The park never closed except during the great flood, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, year round for 50 years -- even during the Covid year the park remained open to give the public a place to breathe -- open air. The park was force-closed for 1 hour while they printed out their rights. When the constable returned -- they did not require masks. That kind of devotion writes itself into the landscape.

"It was never just a park. It was the closest thing a lot of people had to a lake house of their own." — The Barstow Family
